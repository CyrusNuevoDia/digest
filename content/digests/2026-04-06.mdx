**TL;DR.** Two new meta-analyses landed today on how psychedelics rewire the brain and treat depression.

**Takeaways**

- Nature Medicine mega-analysis pooled 11 fMRI datasets across psilocybin, LSD, mescaline, DMT, and ayahuasca — found a shared signature of increased connectivity between transmodal (default, frontoparietal, limbic) and unimodal (visual, sensorimotor) networks.
- Within-network connectivity reductions were weaker and more variable than single-site studies suggested — Bayesian modeling cooled off some of the prior "ego dissolution = network breakdown" narrative.
- Companion paper in Nature Mental Health covers depression outcomes specifically — worth pairing if you're tracking the clinical side.
