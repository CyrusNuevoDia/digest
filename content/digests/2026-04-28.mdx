**TL;DR.** Quant firms like Jane Street and Citadel pay $336K–$1.4M because they hire math and CS brains, not finance majors — and the path in is a structured probability/stats stack, not market intuition.

**Takeaways**

- Top firms explicitly don't require finance backgrounds; 2/3 of Jane Street's recent intern class studied CS or math.
- Four roles to know: Quant Researcher (highest paid), Quant Trader, Quant Developer, Risk Quant — with ML/AI roles growing fastest.
- Learn in order: probability (Bayes, EV) → statistics (hypothesis testing, signal vs. noise). Skipping foundations kills you in interviews and live trading.
- Prediction markets are the new frontier — same systematic frameworks as equities, applied to elections, sports, and geopolitics.
