**TL;DR.** In the post-industrial economy, your scattered interests are an asset — but only if you build a "vessel" to channel them into income-generating work.

**Takeaways**

- Specialization was an Industrial Age trick; today it makes you replaceable and dependent on institutions that don't need you specifically.
- The three ingredients of individual sovereignty: self-education, self-interest, and self-sufficiency.
- Tutorial hell and "learning" feel productive but change nothing without a vessel to channel curiosity into output.
- Stop niching down to please the algorithm — the next 2-3 years reward generalists who can build their own platform.
