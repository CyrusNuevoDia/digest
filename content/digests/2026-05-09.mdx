**TL;DR.** Shopify built River, an AI coding agent that *only* works in public Slack channels — turning the company into a German-style "Lehrwerkstatt" where everyone learns by watching each other prompt.

**Takeaways**

- River authored ~1 in 8 PRs merged into Shopify's monorepo last week (1,870 PRs); 5,938 employees used it across 4,450 channels in 30 days.
- The key design choice: River refuses DMs. Forcing AI use into the open made conversations searchable and turned osmosis learning into the default.
- Reframes the AI-deskilling fear: the risk isn't that AI does the work, it's that it does the work *in private* where no one else learns from it.
