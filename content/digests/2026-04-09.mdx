**TL;DR.** Cold email works when it's short, specific, and makes saying yes effortless — most people just never ask.

**Takeaways**

- Five-line formula: hook, why it matters to them, ask, low-effort CTA, sign off. If you can't fit it, you don't understand your offer.
- Kill "hope you're doing well" and "would love to connect" — replace with specific asks like "got 5 min for a quick yes/no?"
- Follow-ups close more deals than first emails. Best follow-up isn't a nudge, it's news (e.g., "we just hit $10k MRR").
- "Worth a quick look or should I close this out?" — giving people an easy out makes them more likely to say yes.

**TL;DR.** A zen parable about a rich man who converts his fortune to worthless white stones — the punchline isn't anti-wealth, it's that rock-bottom despair is just experience, and noticing that is the doorway.

**Takeaways**

- The "enlightenment" moment isn't transcendence — it's the man realizing the worst feeling of his life is just sensation, the thing he spent decades avoiding.
- Reframe for low moments: "is this a potential moment of awakening?" — not a formula, just a pointer to check in when you're down in the shitter.
- Universal oneness, even if true, is "dreadfully boring and disappointing" — the honest take that makes the rest land.
