**TL;DR.** Stupid thinking is one-dimensional, reductive, tribal, and stops asking questions; genius thinking holds threatening ideas as possibilities and traverses the full web of knowledge with width, depth, and height.

**Takeaways**

- "Knowing is horizontal, understanding is vertical" — experts accumulate facts in a domain; genius upgrades the cognitive operating system itself.
- The "smart but dumb" trap: horizontally advanced but vertically stuck (rich-but-miserable founder, talented-but-broke artist, jacked-but-lonely meathead).
- Genius isn't first-principles or systems thinking jargon — it's refusing to stop thinking once you hit the edge of what you already know.
