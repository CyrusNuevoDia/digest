**TL;DR.** Contracts are less about legal lock-in and more about psychological conversion—lower the signing barrier and prospects start *feeling* like customers, which is when they actually become them.

**Takeaways**

- Split the funnel: "sign contract → become customer → start paying → start using" beats forcing all of it to happen at once. Month-to-month pilots convert via inertia + good service.
- Apply the same psychological buy-in pattern beyond sales: asking all 500+ customers for testimonials turns them into champions (30% higher referrals, ~2x NRR).
- Top-of-funnel hires get stuck in the middle—"should I actually jump?" Lowering the commitment threshold (small yeses first) works the same way it does with enterprise contracts.
